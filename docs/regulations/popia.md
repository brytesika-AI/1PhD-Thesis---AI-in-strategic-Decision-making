# Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) - Summary for AI-SRF

## Core Mandate
POPIA aims to protect the right to privacy of natural and juristic persons by safeguarding their personal information.

## Strategic Constraints for AI Systems
1. **Section 72 (Transborder Information Flows)**: Personal information may not be transferred across borders unless the recipient is subject to a law or agreement that provides an adequate level of protection.
   - *AI-SRF Implication*: Off-shore cloud processing of PII requires explicit risk assessment and potentially Information Regulator approval.
2. **Section 13 (Purpose Specification)**: Personal information must be collected for a specific, explicitly defined and lawful purpose.
   - *AI-SRF Implication*: AI models cannot "recycle" data for secondary training without new consent.
3. **Section 19 (Security Safeguards)**: Responsible parties must secure the integrity and confidentiality of personal information in their possession.
   - *AI-SRF Implication*: Algorithmic security is a legal requirement, not just a technical goal.

## Key Compliance Checkpoints
- **Data Minimisation**: Is the AI processing only the minimum data required?
- **Data Subject Rights**: Can the system identify and remove a specific individual's data upon request?
- **Condition 8 (Accountability)**: The board remains legally responsible for the AI's data processing activities.
