# King IV Report on Corporate Governance - Technology and Information

## Principle 12: Technology and Information Governance
The board should assume responsibility for the governance of technology and information by setting the direction for how technology and information should be approached and addressed in the organisation.

## Strategic Constraints for AI Implementation
1. **Non-Delegable Oversight**: Accountability for technology governance cannot be delegated to IT or vendors. The board must possess the literacy to interrogate AI strategic outputs.
2. **Ethical Foundations**: Technology should be used in a way that respects the firm's ethical commitments. AI "Sovereignty" is a requirement for meeting King IV obligations.
3. **Business Resilience**: The board must ensure technology is resilient enough to sustain the business under disruption.
   - *AI-SRF Implication*: The "Infrastructure Autonomy Ratio" (IAR) is a direct measure of King IV compliance in volatile environments.

## Governance Checkpoints
- **Oversight**: Does the board receive a reasoning trace that they can understand?
- **Risk Integration**: Is AI risk integrated into the enterprise risk management (ERM) framework?
- **Value Creation**: Is technology being used to generate long-term value, rather than short-term gains at the cost of resilience?
